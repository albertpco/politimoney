# PolitiMoney Project Stance

PolitiMoney is an open public-record intelligence tool for American government.
It helps people inspect who funds officials, what interests lobby government,
how representatives vote, where public money flows, and where the evidence does
or does not support a claim.

## Civic Principle

Public officials owe their duty to the Constitution, the country, and the people
they represent. PolitiMoney does not label motives, loyalties, or intent. It gives
users the records needed to evaluate whether funding, votes, lobbying,
contracts, and outcomes align with that duty.

## Product Standard

- Show public records, not narrative conclusions.
- Prefer complete rankings and comparable context over isolated examples.
- Separate observed linkage from inferred motive.
- Make sources, methods, freshness, and uncertainty visible.
- Let users query the evidence through the web UI, MCP tools, or their own LLMs.
- Treat incomplete data as a first-class state, not a footnote.

## Claim Boundary

PolitiMoney can support questions like:

- Who funds this official?
- Which committees, donors, lobbyists, agencies, or contractors appear in the record?
- How did this official vote after those records appeared?
- How does this entity compare with the full ranked field?
- What source supports this statement?

PolitiMoney should not assert:

- That a public official has a particular private motive.
- That a donor caused a vote without evidence that meets that standard.
- That funding, lobbying, or foreign registration alone proves allegiance.
- That absence of data proves absence of influence.

The product can be sharp because the records are sharp. It stays defensible by
making the evidence trail inspectable.
